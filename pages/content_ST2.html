2.1 INTRODUCTION: Representation of Women & Marginalized Genders in Leadership Roles:


To reduce inequalities and drive systematic change women’s leadership is essential in achieving SDG 5 and overall progress of the global goal. However, Indigenous women, women from diverse backgrounds, LGBTQ+ individuals often face additional barriers for leadership positions in various Fildes such as business, politics, finance.

Real world example1:
• Kebone Moloko, Founder of Q network support African queer women and non-binary entrepreneurs are provided with equitable business opportunities.

Real world example2:
• Mary Robinson , co-founder of project dandelions which is a women led climate active initiative to emphasize women’s role in environmental leadership

Real world example3:
• Emmanuelle Larroque The CEO of Social Builder who works to close the digital gender divide ensuring women’s leadership in technical world

2.2 THE Impact of Diverse Leadership on Businesses, Education, and Politics:


Women led actions are vital in bringing innovations, inclusivity, better decision making in all sectors and to prioritize sustainability, equal rights, long-term progression. Additionally, Investing in women’s education and training creates Inclusive learning environments through women representation in education field where future generations can be inspired as well. Moreover, Better workplace environments and outperformed financial sectors can be created through companies with more women in power. Furthermore, Women are pushed for better health research and investments to ensure equal sports rights in athletics to prevent injuries like ACL tears.

Real world example1:
• A film maker and an activist named Kely Nasimento highlights the gender gap in sports leadership where women are struggling to find competitive opportunities

Real world example2:
• Google.org Imapact challenge funded organizations are empowering women and girls to prove financial investment quicken gender equality

2.3 HOW Institutions Can Encourage Equal Opportunities for Leadership:


To achieve leadership equalities institutions must implement polices that promote diverse and inclusion, providing financial support in mentorship and destroy system barriers such as wage gaps and discrimination.

Real world example1:
• Japan’s only STEM education charity for women called Waffle offers tech leadership programs for middle and high school girls helping bridge the gender gap in tech leadership.

Real world example2:
• The solutions project led by Gloria Walton fights historical funding biases to ensure women led climate organizations to receive acceptable resources.

Real world example3:
• Marlene Slemberger the CEO of Native women launch, provides online courses and mentorships to help indigenous women in leadership

2.5 CONCLUSION:

In conclusion, Lack of women and marginalized genders in leadership limits overall progress and allow inequality systems. However, Fields like business, finance, sports, education , politics can be improved by diverse leadership producing outcomes such as innovations, inclusivity, sustainability . Therefore, Supportive policies, proper investment in education, mentorships, financial resources are key factors to break barriers and to ensure leadership equality. Finally, successful case studies represent the factor that women led initiatives empowers community , economy and to create lasting impact
